Care Assistant / Support Workers & Home Carers

Joelma Care Agency

Position:

  • Full Care Assistant / Support Workers & Home Carers

Working Hours:

  • 38.30hrs/week on shift rota (Mon - Fri including weekends)

Wages:

  • £20,500-£21,000 / per Annual

SOC CODE: 6145

Job Descriptiton:

  • The Care Assistant provides care and resources to the service user, so that they may continue to live in their homes and community. Home Care may include transportation, house cleaning, personal hygiene, providing meals, and other health and wellness related activities
  • The Care Assistant will ensure all Home Care is provided in a caring and respectful way keeping with all relevant policies and procedures of Joelma Ltd.
  • Failure to provide adequate services may put the service user and others at risk or force service users to move from their homes before they are prepared to do so.
  • As we are also a recruitment agency carers can be sent to cover shifts with other care companies
  • When they are covering shifts with other care establishments, they must comply with the establishments policies and procedures, including following the persons centered care plan.

Duties:

  • Provide Home Care to service users and others in the community with identified needs.
  • Provide Home Care including general housekeeping, personal hygiene and meals
  • Dressing service users.
  • Feeding at meal times, as well as logging food and fluid intake for service users records.
  • Changing bed linen.
  • Making service users feel safe and secure in their surroundings.
  • Transport service users to appointments and activities.
  • Follow medical instructions carefully and consistently.
  • Organize time and resources based on individual needs of service users.
  • Manage any unanticipated events or unstable situations.
  • Identify persons requiring home care and client needs.
  • Ensure care is provided according to all relevant policies and procedures.
  • Discuss any concerns with supervisor.

Physical and Emotional Demands:

  • The Care Assistant is required to safely lift and move clients, and may be required to bathe or clean clients. The worker may be required to administer interventions and treatments that cause discomfort to the client or to themselves. The worker will work in the service users home and must practice clean, safe care to eliminate the possibility of infection or cross contaminate between clients, family members or themselves
  • Care Assistants are faced with stress from dealing with clients and family members who may be stressed and difficult to work with. Stress is caused by the fact that the worker must work independently in unfamiliar and uncontrolled situations. There is significant emotional stress in providing care for clients who are dying and providing support for families.

Working Conditions:

  • Care Assistants work in the homes of service users, which will vary in terms of cleanliness, warmth, physical structure and material standards.
  • Care Assistants may be required to undertake other tasks specified by their manager relevant to the role and to the needs of the service users
  • Care Assistant must not use equipment that is unsafe. Any unsafe equipment to be reported to the line manager immediately.

Skills and Attributes:

  • An interest in helping other people, regardless of their condition
  • Ability to cope under pressure
  • The ability to communicate clearly and sensitively when talking to people and their families
  • Good listening skills
  • Time management skills
  • The ability to work both alone and as part of a team
  • A high level of patience and emotional resilience
  • Being empathetic towards everyone
  • The ability to make good, positive relationships with people and their families
  • The ability to communicate with other healthcare professionals
  • Great verbal and written communication skills
  • A non-judgemental attitute regardless of a person's needs.
  • The ability to remain calm under pressure when dealing with challenging situations

Qualifications / Work Experience:

  • Mininum of RQF level 2 in health & social care or relevant trainings such as care certificate or the evidence of trainings done in the manadatory.
  • Experience working as a care assistants or an auxilliary nursing assistant.
